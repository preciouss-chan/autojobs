# Resume Tailoring Before & After Comparison

## Test Case: Senior Full-Stack Engineer Role (Next.js, React, TypeScript)

This document shows the real-world impact of the resume tailoring feature by comparing the original resume with a version tailored for a specific job posting.

---

## Executive Summary

The tailoring engine successfully:
- ✅ **Rewrote the professional summary** to highlight relevant tech stack (React, Next.js, TypeScript)
- ✅ **Shortened experience bullets** from 5 bullets to 2 focused bullets aligned with job requirements
- ✅ **Refined project descriptions** to emphasize full-stack architecture and efficiency metrics
- ✅ **Suggested missing skills** to add (TypeScript, Next.js, React, CI/CD, Docker)
- ✅ **Generated a tailored cover letter** (300 words) that directly references job requirements and specific achievements

---

## Key Findings

### What Worked Well ✅

1. **Smart Filtering** - AI correctly identified that VR/ML skills don't apply to web engineering role
2. **Metric Preservation** - Kept quantifiable achievements (40%, 200+, 90%)
3. **Tech Stack Alignment** - Rewrote bullets to emphasize React/Next.js/TypeScript
4. **Skill Suggestions** - Recommended exactly the missing skills from job posting (TypeScript, Next.js, CI/CD, Docker)
5. **Cover Letter Quality** - Generated a professional, personalized letter with specific references

### Opportunities for Improvement ⚠️

1. **Experience Reduction** - Shortened VR role from 5 to 2 bullets (lost detail about VM configuration, hardware troubleshooting)
   - Trade-off: More focused on relevant skills, but loses some context about technical depth
2. **Award Removal** - Removed DevHacks award mention (could have kept for credibility)
   - Reason: LLM prioritized tech skills over awards
3. **Years of Experience** - Cover letter claims "2 years experience" but resume shows July 2025-present (recent grad)
   - Bug: LLM made assumption about tenure
